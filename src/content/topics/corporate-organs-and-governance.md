## Introduction

This unit treats the company as a decision-making architecture. Students identify who has authority, how meetings are convened, when resolutions are vulnerable, and how the 2023 revision permits more flexible organ design.

## Key Legal Issues

- Allocation of powers among shareholders, directors, managers, supervisors, and audit committees.
- Meeting notice, voting, minutes, and challengeable resolutions.
- Employee representation and worker-facing governance rules.
- State-invested company governance as a special institutional variant.

## Hypotheticals

- The board removes a general manager after a short meeting and the manager challenges the reasons.
- A company replaces its board of supervisors with an audit committee but forgets to revise its articles.
- A large company with more than 300 employees has no employee director or employee supervisor.

## Legislation

Use the Company Law's organ provisions and state-invested company chapter. Interpretation IV helps frame resolution disputes and information rights. State-owned asset rules add the state-investor layer. Listed-company governance, articles, and independent-director instruments show how public-company governance becomes more regulated. Employee democratic-management rules supply the worker-participation context.

## Introduction

This unit focuses on the 2023 revision's renewed concern with capital credibility. Students map who promised what, when payment is due, who can accelerate payment, and how creditors use capital rules.

## Key Legal Issues

- Subscription, contribution periods, transitional adjustment, and Article 88 equity-transfer liability.
- False contribution, late contribution, withdrawal of capital, forfeiture, and director collection responsibility.
- Debt set-off, non-monetary contributions, capital reduction, distribution constraints, and creditor notice.
- How capital rules interact with registration, contract claims, insolvency, enforcement, and comparative legal-capital systems.

## Hypotheticals

- Shareholders agree to a 30-year contribution period before the revised law takes effect.
- A company reduces registered capital while a supplier's invoice remains unpaid.
- A director knows a contribution is unpaid but approves a distribution anyway.

## Legislation

The Company Law provides the contribution framework. The State Council registered-capital provisions, SPC temporal-effect rules, and the Article 88 non-retroactivity reply are essential for transitional disputes. Interpretation III, the 2025 draft interpretation, the Jiu Min Minutes, registration rules, the Bankruptcy Law, state-owned asset transaction rules, the Civil Code, and the EU directive help students connect unpaid capital to creditor remedies, equity transfers, public filings, and comparative legal-capital design.

## Cases

Guiding Case No. 9 is useful because capital and liquidation records become creditor-protection issues once a company can no longer satisfy ordinary debts. The added cases then move from internal contribution-period resolutions, to set-off of shareholder claims, to malicious transfers of unpaid subscribed capital, and finally to foreign-investor contribution duties.

## Readings

The readings trace China's movement from 2014 capital liberalization to renewed contribution discipline in the 2023 revision. Use the Chinese scholarship to test contribution acceleration, debt-law framing, and shareholder limited liability; use the practice notes for implementation; and use the EU legal-capital materials to ask whether registered capital is meant to inform creditors, discipline shareholders, or perform both functions.

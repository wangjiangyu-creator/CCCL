## Introduction

This unit treats formation as both private ordering and public administration. Students learn how articles, registered items, legal representatives, business scope, and public records frame later disputes.

## Key Legal Issues

- Promoter liability and pre-incorporation transactions.
- Mandatory and optional content of articles of association.
- Registration effects, amendment procedures, and reliance on public records.
- Foreign-invested enterprise organization after the Foreign Investment Law.

## Hypotheticals

- A promoter signs a lease before registration and later tells the landlord to sue the new company only.
- Articles restrict transfers among employee-shareholders, but one employee leaves and challenges the repurchase clause.
- A foreign investor wants a China subsidiary and asks which steps are corporate-law steps and which are investment-control steps.

## Legislation

Use the Company Law for formation, articles, legal representatives, and registered capital. Use the market-entity registration regulation and implementing rules for filings, amendments, and public-record effects. Use the Foreign Investment Law, its implementing regulation, information reporting measures, and the current negative list when formation involves foreign investors.

## Cases

Guiding Case No. 96 tests how far articles and initial shareholder consent can regulate exit, transfer, and repurchase arrangements in a restructured company.

## Readings

The practice readings give a quick map of the revised Company Law and the foreign-investment reporting system. The capital-registration readings help students see why formation documents now matter for later creditor and contribution disputes.

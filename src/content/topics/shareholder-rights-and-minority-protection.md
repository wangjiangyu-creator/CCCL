## Introduction

This unit moves from shareholder ownership to shareholder remedies. Students identify which rights can be exercised directly, which belong to the company, and when a minority shareholder can force judicial intervention.

## Key Legal Issues

- Information and inspection rights.
- Resolution validity, revocation, and nullity.
- Profit distribution and repurchase rights.
- Derivative litigation, representative litigation, and dissolution.
- Transfer restrictions in close companies.

## Hypotheticals

- A minority shareholder asks to inspect accounts after suspecting related-party diversion.
- Majority shareholders refuse dividends for several profitable years while paying insiders through service contracts.
- A 50 percent shareholder seeks dissolution after all meetings fail.

## Legislation

Use the Company Law with Interpretations II, III, IV, and V for information rights, resolution challenges, contribution-linked disputes, distributions, derivative claims, and dissolution. Securities and CSRC instruments add listed-company minority protection, while UK and Hong Kong statutes supply comparative reference points.

## Cases

Guiding Case No. 8 addresses dissolution as a deadlock remedy. Guiding Case No. 96 tests article-based restrictions. Foss v. Harbottle explains why derivative actions are exceptional.

## Readings

The SPC press materials are useful for seeing the recurring practical disputes behind Interpretation IV. The inspection-rights paper sharpens the information-rights problem, while the OECD and fiduciary-duty readings connect minority protection to governance design and enforcement.

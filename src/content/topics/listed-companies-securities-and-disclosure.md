## Introduction

This unit expands from private company governance to capital markets. Students ask how disclosure, exchange listing, investor protection, and cross-border offerings change the governance problem.

## Key Legal Issues

- Listed-company special governance rules under the Company Law.
- Securities disclosure, false statement liability, and investor protection.
- Overseas listing filing for domestic companies and red-chip structures.
- Independent directors, board committees, and compliance monitoring.

## Hypotheticals

- A domestic company restructures through an offshore holding company and submits a Hong Kong listing application.
- Directors learn of a material related-party loan but the announcement omits it.
- A public company's compliance system misses repeated safety warnings.

## Legislation

Use the Company Law for internal listed-company governance, the Securities Law for disclosure and market liability, and the CSRC overseas listing measures for cross-border offerings. Add the 2026 governance code, disclosure measures, articles guidelines, independent-director measures, and security-review rules for current public-company practice.

## Cases

Caremark and Van Gorkom provide comparative ways to think about board oversight, information, and process in public-company settings.

## Readings

The OECD materials give a policy-oriented view of listed-company governance in China and internationally. The empirical duty readings help connect disclosure failures to board process, while the bankruptcy and foreign-investment policy notes show how public-company governance intersects with distress and cross-border regulation.

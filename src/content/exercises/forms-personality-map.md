## Facts

Three graduates start a consulting business in Shenzhen. They use one trade name, split profits equally, and sign two contracts before deciding whether to register a company. One founder later registers a limited liability company using the same trade name.

## Questions

1. Who is likely liable on the pre-registration contracts?
2. What changes after the company is registered?
3. Which facts would make a creditor argue that the company is only a shell?

## Hints

Start by separating the time before registration from the time after registration. Then list which assets, names, records, and bank accounts belong to which person or entity.

## Discussion Guide

Students should produce a timeline and an entity map. The strongest answers distinguish formation liability from later separate personality and identify facts relevant to veil piercing without assuming that every small company is abusive.

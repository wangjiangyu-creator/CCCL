## Facts

The board of a medium-sized company holds a 20-minute online meeting to remove the general manager, approve a related-party service contract, and replace the supervisor with an audit committee. The minutes are short and only two directors sign.

## Questions

1. Which decisions belong to which corporate organ?
2. What procedural defects might make a resolution challengeable?
3. What changes, if any, must be reflected in the articles?

## Hints

Separate authority, notice, quorum, voting, minutes, signatures, and articles. A commercially poor decision is not automatically an illegal decision.

## Discussion Guide

Students should use the facts to classify each decision. The discussion should connect corporate autonomy with procedural discipline and ask what a court can review.

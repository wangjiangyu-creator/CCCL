## Facts

A company was registered in 2022 with RMB 50 million subscribed capital and a 30-year payment period. By August 2025 only RMB 5 million has been paid. A supplier has an unpaid judgment and asks whether shareholder contribution obligations can help.

## Questions

1. What dates matter under the revised Company Law and temporal-effect rules?
2. Which shareholder obligations should be checked first?
3. What facts would matter to a creditor-facing argument?

## Hints

Build the answer around time, amount, due date, company solvency, and the identity of the person asking for payment.

## Discussion Guide

Students should avoid a simple yes-or-no answer. They should identify transition issues, possible acceleration or adjustment questions, and the difference between company claims against shareholders and creditor routes.

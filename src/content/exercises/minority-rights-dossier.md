## Facts

A 20 percent shareholder has not received dividends for four years. The company reports profits but pays large consulting fees to entities linked to the majority shareholder. The minority shareholder requests accounting records and plans litigation.

## Questions

1. Which rights can the shareholder exercise directly?
2. Which claims might belong to the company rather than the shareholder personally?
3. What evidence should be requested before choosing a remedy?

## Hints

Separate inspection rights, profit distribution, resolution challenges, derivative claims, and dissolution. Ask what injury each remedy addresses.

## Discussion Guide

Students should identify a staged strategy: information first, then classification of wrong, then remedy. The exercise should not reveal a model litigation plan.

## Facts

A local state-owned enterprise forms a joint venture with a foreign investor to manufacture specialized components. The state investor wants veto rights over major assets. The foreign investor wants ordinary company-law governance and free transfer rights.

## Questions

1. Which issues are Company Law issues?
2. Which issues are state-owned asset or foreign investment issues?
3. How should the articles allocate authority without ignoring mandatory rules?

## Hints

Make three columns: company governance, state asset supervision, and foreign investment control. Then test where the same fact appears in more than one column.

## Discussion Guide

Students should identify the tension between private ordering and public-law constraints. The exercise should encourage careful drafting instincts without requiring a final transaction structure.

## Facts

A PRC technology company with an offshore holding structure plans a Hong Kong listing. Before filing, the domestic operating company enters a large loan with an entity controlled by the founder. The board minutes describe the loan as ordinary working-capital support.

## Questions

1. Which company-law issues arise before any securities filing?
2. Which securities or overseas-listing issues should be flagged?
3. What board process and disclosure facts would you want to verify?

## Hints

Track the domestic operating company, offshore issuer, founder controller, related-party transaction, board approval, and public-market disclosure separately.

## Discussion Guide

Students should connect internal approval with external disclosure. The goal is to see how a private governance defect can become a public-market problem.

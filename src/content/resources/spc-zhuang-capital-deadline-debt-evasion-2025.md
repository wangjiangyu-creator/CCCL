## Brief English Introduction

A shareholder with a large unpaid subscription transferred equity for zero consideration to affiliated actors after the company could not satisfy a judgment debt. The transferee then extended the contribution deadline to 2040. The court held the extension ineffective against the creditor and imposed supplementary liability within the unpaid contribution amount.

## Use It For

Use this for Article 54 acceleration, Article 88-style transfer problems, debt evasion, contribution-period extensions, and the relationship between shareholder autonomy and creditor reliance.

## Teaching Notes

The case is a strong reminder that contribution timing is not only an internal bargain. Once the company cannot pay, zero-price affiliated transfers and deadline extensions can become creditor-harm facts.

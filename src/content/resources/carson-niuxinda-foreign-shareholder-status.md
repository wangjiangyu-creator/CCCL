## Brief English Introduction

Carson v. Niuxinda shows how the Foreign Investment Law changed the court's review of foreign dormant-shareholder disputes. Once the actual investor had contributed, the other shareholders consented, and the business was outside the negative list, the court supported registration of the foreign natural person as shareholder.

## Use It For

Use this case when formation and registration questions overlap with foreign-investment access control.

## Teaching Notes

The case prevents students from treating every foreign-invested-company issue as an approval problem; the negative list determines whether extra access scrutiny is needed.

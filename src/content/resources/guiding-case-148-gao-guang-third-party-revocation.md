## Brief English Introduction

The case separates the shareholder's interest in the company from the company's own litigation position. A shareholder cannot treat every adverse company judgment as a direct personal injury.

## Use It For

Use this case when discussing separate legal personality, derivative litigation, and the difference between shareholder reflective loss and direct shareholder rights.

## Teaching Notes

It is a useful counterweight to expansive shareholder-remedy arguments: company personality protects both limited liability and procedural boundaries.

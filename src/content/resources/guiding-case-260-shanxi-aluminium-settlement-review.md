## Brief English Introduction

The defendant aluminium company stored red mud in a large residue reservoir that created pollution and safety risks. The court refused to endorse a weak settlement that did not solve the environmental problem, then approved a mediated plan only after it contained concrete closure work, timing, funding, inspection, and supervision terms.

## Use It For

Use this case when discussing whether corporate environmental responsibility can be privately settled away.

## Teaching Notes

Students should test the difference between formal settlement and public-interest repair. In environmental public-interest litigation, the court must ask whether the agreement actually restores or protects the public interest.

## Brief English Introduction

The claimant supplied vegetables to a restaurant company and alleged that the controlling shareholder's personal assets and the company's assets were commingled. The court's key lesson is temporal: once commingling has harmed the company's solvency and creditor interests, later transferring the shares does not cleanse the shareholder's responsibility.

## Use It For

Use this case when students ask whether a shareholder can exit liability through a later transfer.

## Teaching Notes

The case makes causation and timing visible. Students should ask when the abusive conduct occurred, when the creditor's loss became likely, and whether the transfer changed the creditor's position.

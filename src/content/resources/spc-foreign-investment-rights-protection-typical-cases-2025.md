## Use It For

Use this to show how ordinary company-law doctrines protect foreign investors and FIEs after the Foreign Investment Law: fiduciary-like control of managers, information rights, deadlock dissolution, preservation measures, and related-party transaction liability.

## Teaching Notes

The release is especially useful because the cases are not framed as market-access cases. They show national treatment in litigation: foreign-invested companies use ordinary company-law remedies, but the court also signals protection of foreign-investment expectations.

## Brief English Introduction

The case challenged a hydropower project that could affect the habitat of endangered green peafowl. The Supreme People's Court emphasized prevention: when evidence shows a real risk of destructive and irreversible ecological harm, courts should evaluate the protected interest, likelihood, seriousness, and irreversibility of the damage before waiting for harm to occur.

## Use It For

Use this as a similar case for the preventive dimension of corporate environmental responsibility.

## Teaching Notes

The case lets students test how far corporate approvals and development goals should protect a company when public-interest plaintiffs allege grave ecological risk.

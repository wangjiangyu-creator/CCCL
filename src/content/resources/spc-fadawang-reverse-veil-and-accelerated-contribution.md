## Brief English Introduction

This official Q&A is useful because it addresses two live post-2023 issues: whether Chinese law should recognize reverse corporate personality denial, and whether a creditor may request direct payment from a shareholder whose contribution obligation has accelerated.

## Use It For

Use it after the Jiu Min Minutes to test how courts should handle reverse-piercing arguments, substantive consolidation, shareholder-creditor claims, and direct creditor claims against unpaid capital.

## Teaching Notes

The answer narrows reverse veil piercing to personality-confusion situations while preserving ordinary creditor tools such as equity execution, revocation, subrogation, bankruptcy, and contribution claims.
